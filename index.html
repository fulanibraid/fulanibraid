Fulani Braids Maintenance: Complete Care Guide

Good maintenance is what separates braids that still look fresh at week six from braids that look frizzy and worn by week two. The routine itself is simple — it just needs to be consistent. Here's how to keep Fulani braids neat, your scalp healthy, and your style lasting as long as possible.

Daily Care

Apply a lightweight scalp oil to prevent dryness and itching — argan, jojoba, or castor oil all work well without weighing the braids down. Smooth flyaways along the parts with a small amount of gel using a soft brush, and try to avoid touching or restyling the braids too often, since frequent handling loosens the hairline faster.

Nighttime Care

Protect your braids and retain moisture overnight with a satin or silk bonnet, or sleep on a satin pillowcase. This single habit does more to extend the life of the style than almost anything else — it reduces friction, keeps edges smooth, and cuts down on frizz.

Washing

Wash every one to two weeks using a diluted, sulfate-free shampoo applied directly to the scalp with an applicator bottle — focus on the roots rather than scrubbing the braids themselves. Rinse thoroughly and let the braids air dry completely to avoid mildew or odor.

Avoid Product Buildup

Heavy oils and creams can build up at the roots and make braids look greasy or dull. Stick to lightweight, residue-free products and don't over-apply between washes.

How Long Do Fulani Braids Last?

With proper care, Fulani braids typically last 4 to 8 weeks, depending on hair texture, how the braids were installed, and how consistent the maintenance routine is.

Swimming and Workouts

You can still swim or exercise with Fulani braids — just rinse with clean water afterward, especially after chlorine or salt water, and keep braids tied back during workouts to reduce friction and frizz.
